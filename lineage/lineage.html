Standard Lineages

Dragonborn

The ancestors of Dragonborn hatched from the eggs of chromatic and metallic dragons.

Dwarf

Bold and hardy, Dwarves are known as skilled warriors, miners, and workers of stone and metal.

Elf

Elves are a magical people of otherworldly grace, living in the world but not entirely part of it.

Gnome

Gnomes are magical folk created by gods of invention, illusions, and life underground.

Half-Elf

Half-elves combine what some say are the best qualities of their elf and human parents.

Half-Orc

Some Half-Orcs rise to become proud leaders of orc communities. Some venture into the world to prove their worth. Many of these become adventurers, achieving greatness for their mighty deeds.

Halfing

Halflings posses a brave and adventurous spirit that leadds them on journeys of discovery.

Human

Found throughout the multiverse, humans are as varied as they are numerous.

Tiefling

Tieflings are either born in the Lower Planes or have fiendish ancestors who originated there.

Exotic Lineages

Aarakocra

Sequestered in high mountains atop tall trees, the Aarakocra, sometimes called birdfolk, evoke fear and wonder.

Aasimar

Whether descended from a celestial being or infused with heavenly power, Aasimar are mortals who carry a spark of the Upper Planes within their souls.

Bugbear

Despite their formidable build, Bugbears are quite skulkers, thanks to a fey magic that allows them to hide in spaces seemingly too small for them.

Centaur

The upper bodies of Centaurs are comparable to human torsos in size, and their lower equine bodies average about 4 feet tall at the withers. They fill similar roles as cavalry warriors, messengers, outriders, and scouts.

Changeling

For some Changelings, a new face is only a disguise. For others a new face may reveal an aspect of their soul.

Deep Gnome

Deep Gnomes, or Svirfneblin, are natives of the Underdark and are suffused with that subterranean realms' magic.

Duergar

Duergar are dwarves whose ancestors were transformed byu centuries living in the deepest places of the Underdark.

Eladrin

Eladrin are elves of the Feywild, a realm of perilous beauty and boundless magic.

Fairy

Infused with the magic of the Feywild, most Fairies look like small Elves with insectile wings, but each Fairy has a special physical characteristic that sets the Fairy apart.

Firbolg

Distant cousins of giants, the first Firbolgs wandered the primeval forests of the multiverse, and the magic of those forests entwined itself with the Firbolgs' souls.

Genasi (Air)

Embodying many of the airy traits of their otherworldy ancestors, Air Genasi can draw upon their connection to the winds.

Genasi (Earth)

Tracing their ancestory to dao, the genies of the Elemental Plane of Earth, Earth Genasi inherit dao's steadfast strength and control over earth.

Genasi (Fire)

Descened from efreet, the genies of the Elemental Plane of Fire, Fire Genasi channel the flamboyant and often destructive nature of flame.

Genasi (Water)

Water Genasi are perfectly suited to life underwater and carry the power of the waves inside themselves.

Githyanki

Long ago, the Gith rose up to overthrow the Mind Flayers that held them in servitude, but two factions arose that remain bitter enemies today.

Githzerai

Eons of cultivating their ental powers within the endless chaos of Limbo have imbued Githzerai with the ability to shape psionic energy to protect themselves and probe minds.

Goblin

Goblins occupy an uneasy place in a dangerous world, and they react by lashing out at any creatures they believe they can bully.

Goliath

Distantly related to Giants and infused with the supernatual essense of their ancestors' mountainous home.

Grung

Your Grung character has an assortment of inborn abilities, part and parcel of Grung nature.

Harengon

Harengons are blessed with a little fey luck, and they often find themselves a few fortunate feet away from dangers during adventures.

Hobgoblin

War is the lifeblood of Hobgoblins. Its glories are the dreams that inspire them. Its horrors don't feature in their nightmares.

Kenku

Haunted by an ancient crime that robbed them of their wings, the Kenku wander the world as vagabounds and burglars who live at the edge of human society.

Kobold

Kobolds are typically timid and shy away from conflict, but they are dangerous and vicious if cornered.

Lizardfolk

Gifted by the gods with remarkable physical defenses and a mystical connection to the natural world, Lizardfolk can survive with just their wits in situations that would be deadly for other folk.

Locathah

These resilient and proud fish-folk have endured war, slavery, and mistreatment at the hands of other aquatic creatures.

Minotaur

Minotaurs are barrel-chested humanoids with heads resembling those of bulls. Blessed with a supernaturally strong sense of direction, Minotaurs make great navigators.

Orc

Orcs live a life that has no place for weakness, and every warrior must be strong enough to take what is needed by force.

Owlin

Like owls, Owlin are graced with feathers that make no sound when they move or fly, making it easy for them to sneak up on you in the library.

Satyr

Satyrs embody a fusion of humanoid civilization with the freedom of wild beasts.

Sea Elf

Sea Elves fell in love with the wild beauty of the ocean in the earlist days of the multiverse.

Shadar-Kai

Once a Shadar-Kai were Fey like the rest of their elven kin; now they exist in a state between life and death, thanks to being transformed by the Shadowfell.

Shifter

Humanoids with a bestial aspect, shifters can't fully change shape, but they can temporarily enhance their animalistic features by entering a stat they call shifting.

Tabaxi

Created by the Cat Lord - a divine being of the Upper Planes - to blend the qualities of humanoids and cats, Tabaxi are a varied poeple in both attitude and appearance.

Tortle

What many Tortles consider a simple life, others might call a life of adventure. They are nomad survivalists eager to explore the wilderness.

Triton

Long-established guardians of the deep ocean floor, in recent years the noble Tritons have become increasingly active in the world above.

Verdan

The Verdan owe their existence to chaos - doing their best to find their way in an unfamiliar world.

Yuan-Ti

Blessed with resistance to magical and poisonous effects by the rituals that created them, each of these Yuan-Ti manifests their serpentine heritage in a variety of ways.

Setting Specific Lineages

Dragonlance

Kender

Kender have a supernatual curiosity that drives them to adventure.

Eberron

Kalashtar

The Kalashtar are a compound poeple created from the union of humanity and renegade spirits from the Plane of Dreams.

Warforged

Warforged are made from wood and metal, but they can feel pain and emotion. Built as weapons, they must now find a purpose beyond war.

Ravnica

Loxodon

Humanoid elephants, Loxodons are strong, calm, and wise.

Simic Hybrid

The Simic Combine uses magic to transfer the traits of animals into Humans, Elves, and Vedalken.

Vedalken

The blue-skinned Vedalken strive for perfection above all else.

Spelljammer

Astral Elf

Groups of Elves ventured from the Feywild to the Astral Plane to be closer to their gods. Lie in the Silver Void has imbued their souls with a spark of divine light.

Autognome

Sometimes, because of a malfunction or a unique circumstance, an Autognome becomes separated from its creater and strikes out on its own.

Giff

As beings of impressive size and unforgettable appearance, Giff are noticed wherever they go.

Hadozee

Hadozees are sapient, bipedal beings eager to leave behind the fearsome predators of their homeworld and explore other worlds.

Plasmoid

Plasmoids are amorphous beings with no typical shape. In the presence of other folk, they often adopt a similar shpae, but there's little chance of mistaking a Plasmoid for anything else.

Thri-kreen

Thri-kreen speak by clacking their mandibles and waving their antennae, indicating what they are thinking and feeling.

Theros

Leonin

Nomadic, lion-like humanoids who rarely interact with other peoples, having all they need in their shimmering homeland.

Book of Ebon Tides

Bearfolk

All bearfolk share an inherent iron resolve. Only by banding together in tight-knit communities dedicated to fulfilling their noble purpose have the bearfolk miraculously continued to thrive.

Darakhul

The darakhul are no strangers to darkness, and over the years, they deftly adapted to their new home and carved out a thriving civilization in the Shadow Realm.

Erina

The erina are a small-statured species of creatures resembling bipedal hedgehogs.

Quickstep

Quicksteps have a fondness for finery and feel right at home amid the riches of court life. As their name implies, they possess a natural affinity for speed, which makes them exquisitely qualified sabotage and dueling.

Ratatosk

In recent decades, the squirrel-like beings called ratatosk have grown bored with simply safeguarding the World Tree and have decided to more actively battle corruption in the Shadow Realm.

Ravenfolk

Few races in Midgard live amid so much rumor, suspicion, and outright falsehood as the ravenfolk.

Satarre

The satarre are powerful and wise creatures often found serving dark cults or (less often) warning other races of the plans of such cults.

Shade

Life leaves echoes in its wake, whether crumbled remnants of a bygone civilization or even the echo of an individual soul left behind to haunt the living world.

Shadow Goblin

These goblins are closer to fey than to their monstrous cousins in the mortal lands, and their cunning dispositions allow them to integrate into the shadow courts.

Umbral Human

The Shadow Realm twists beings with its magic, and in the case of the umbral humans, this influence can have radical effects.

Grim Hollow

The Disembodied

The Disembodied are the survivors of the Ethereal Rift, trapped between worlds with a tenuous grasp on the Material Plane.

Wechselkind

A wechselkind is a construct crafted of wood, clay, and ceramic in the form of a small child.

Humblewood

Cervan

Cervans are a versatile deer-like folk who make their homes in small, intimate villages throughout Humblewood.

Corvum

Corvums are a crow-like race with dark feathers and sharp minds. Corvums are crafty and they tend to get what they want, one way or another.

Gallus

Gallus have a deep respect for nature and a love of gardening unmatched by other birdfolk. They value their social bonds above all else, and are always ready to help their neighbors.

Hedge

The most diplomatic and good natured of all the humblefolk, hedges like to live quiet lives in villages throughout the Wood, or within the safety of birdfolk perches.

Jerbeen

Jerbeens are small mouse-like folk with long, furred tails that help them balance.

Luma

Lumas are smaller than most birdfolk, and resemble either doves or pigeons. They are as often shunned for their eccentricities as they are celebrated for them.

Mapach

Naturally curious, mapachs are a medium-sized racoon-like race.

Raptor

Resembling small hawks, eagles, and other birds of prey, they have sharp talons and agile builds that help them effortlessly glide through the canopy.

Strig

Resembling owls, their thick feathers, solid bodies, and broad wings have earned them a reputation as the most powerful of the birdfolk races.

Vulpin

One of the least understood and most often vilified humblefolk in the Wood, vulpins have earned a bad reputation that isn’t entirely deserved.

Heliana's Guilde to Monster Hunting

Cnidaran

Cnidarans can become enraptured by wanderlust and spend their days among winding ocean currents, delving into damp dungeons, and cavorting in ports with others.

Cyclopian

The pursuit of knowledge and the acquisition of secrets are common practices among any creature raised by cyclopians.

Gobboc

Nestled in secluded clearings in the woods, on hilltops with extraordinary vantages, or in easily collapsible canyons, gobbocs seek safety above all else.

Golynn

Vivacious wanderers, golynn are creatures of nature and are natural adventurers.

Rakin

Rakin can be found at all levels of society, a product of their ingeniousness and habit of getting into trouble.

Griffon's Saddlebag: Book Two

Etherean

For many ethereans, the relative safety of the Ethereal Plane outweighs the richer experiences of the Material, but for those that do manage to cross that planar threshold, there is simply no comparison.

Geleton

Geletons are the living, symbiotic fusion of two semiconscious life forms: oozes and reanimated skeletons.
